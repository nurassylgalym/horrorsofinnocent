"Protests and Terrorist Organizations in Syria"

The civil war in Syria, which began in 2011, gave rise to numerous protests and demonstrations. This conflict situation compelled Syrian citizens to take to the streets to express their discontent and demands for the government.

One of the most well-known protests occurred in March 2011 in the city of Daraa. Protesters demanded the release of political prisoners and political reforms. However, this peaceful protest was brutally suppressed, leading to further protests and an escalation of the conflict. Over time, many other protests and mass actions spread across Syria. Protesters made diverse demands, including freedom of speech, free elections, and the departure of President Bashar al-Assad. These protests often escalated into violence and armed actions.

As for terrorist organizations in Syria, one of the most infamous was the "Islamic State Group" (ISIS). This extremist organization entered Syria in 2013 and began seizing territories, such as Raqqa, conducting ruthless attacks, and establishing control over various cities and regions. Their goal was to establish an Islamic caliphate. Another significant group was "Al-Qaeda in Syria," known as "Jabhat al-Nusra." This organization also actively participated in the armed conflict, fighting against the government and other opposition forces.

The Syrian civil war has caused immense suffering for the local population and had global repercussions. Protests and terrorist organizations have become an integral part of this bloody conflict situation, which continues to this day...

"No one is forgotten, nothing is forgotten"

Children in Syria face incredible hardships while living in a country torn apart by war and conflicts. This conflict, which began in 2011, has left children without a normal childhood and access to basic rights...

Many children in Syria grew up in constant fear and distress. The sounds of explosions and gunfire became a part of their daily lives. Over 30% of children lost their parents, friends, and homes due to the fighting. This has led to serious psychological trauma and stress for many of them.

Education also suffers in the conditions of war. Many schools and nurseries have been destroyed, and children are deprived of access to education. Most of them are forced to remain illiterate, seriously undermining their future prospects and aspirations.

In addition, children and adolescents face a lack of basic necessities such as food and medical aid. Hunger and disease have become a part of their daily lives, and many of them suffer from malnutrition and preventable illnesses.

Despite all these hardships, children in Syria display incredible resilience and determination. They dream of a peaceful and better future, and many of them continue to hope for a brighter tomorrow, despite the horrors of this civil war.

Syrian children require assistance and support from the global community. They deserve safety, education, and the opportunity to realize their potential. Their resilience and hope for the future should inspire all of us because all children in this world deserve love and care.

Syria invested in education, and the level of education was relatively high, with access to healthcare ensured.

Residents walked the streets at night without much fear. Women and men had equal opportunities in employment and education, among other things. "Testimony of Mrs. Zhu Gao"

Civil War in Syria... Since the beginning of the civil war in Syria in 2011, the country has faced destruction, human suffering, and global consequences. The conflict has affected every aspect of Syrians' lives and has caused significant changes in world politics. Millions of Syrians were forced to leave their homes due to bombings and violence. Internal displacement and refugees became a massive problem. Many of them encountered shortages of food, water, and medical assistance.

Furthermore, the war has led to the destruction of a significant part of the country's infrastructure, including schools, hospitals, and roads. This left Syrians without access to basic services and created a humanitarian catastrophe. As a result, one-third of Syrians lost their jobs and homes.

What were the geopolitical consequences in Syria? The conflict in Syria attracted the attention of numerous world powers, including Russia, the United States, Turkey, and others. This resulted in complex interactions and external interests, including support for different sides of the conflict.

In 2014-15, terrorist organizations such as ISIS began to actively operate in Syria. The conflict created conditions for their growth and expansion, posing a serious threat to the region and the world.

Syrians continue to suffer from this war, and the resolution of the conflict remains one of the main global priorities. The international community must collaborate to achieve peace and assist those affected.
